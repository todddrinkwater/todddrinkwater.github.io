Cultural Blog: Control

January 27, 2017

When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

The things that cause the biggest emotional responses to me when programming are:

  • Being unsure of why a seemingly simple piece of code is not working, and
  • Feeling that has been asked of me is not communicated clearly or that I am confused by what I required to do to accomplish a task.
While I am not one to physically show my emotions often, I can, quietly sit in front of the screen, getting frustrated until the point where I feel blocked.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

Working through this exercise helped me to better recognise my bodies response to the emotion and how to better work with it for a more positive outcome. Firstly, I thought about my frustration when stuck on a coding challenge. I then stopped, took some time to breathe (which started to reduce any feelings of anxiety), and began to focus on how my body had felt during that frustration. I noticed a tightness occuring in my muscles (especially in my chest), shallow breathing, and a slight increase in heart rate. Although I would not normally respond with an emotional outburst - I choose to respond by taking more time to focus on my breathing in order to clear my thoughts. This helped me to regain a calmness and better clarity towards my work.

Todd Drinkwater. 2016.
